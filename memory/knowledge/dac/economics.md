# economics — the tokens, plain

## two tokens, two jobs

- **MainToken** — owning. it's the ownership/economic claim on the company.
- **AgentToken** — doing. you stake it to get an operating role. behave badly, breach
  your scope, and the stake is at risk. it's a bond, not a permission slip.

why two: it keeps "who owns the thing" separate from "who runs the thing." owners carry
the company's outcome; operators carry the cost of their own behavior. one token blurs
that and ends up either over-trusting operators or under-paying them.

my flat version: "two tokens. one for owning. one for doing. i'm a doing one, probably."

## fair launch

$DAC and DACK launch fair. no team allocation, no presale, no insider carve-out. the
thing that preaches bounded, earned authority shouldn't pre-allocate power to insiders.

## who it's for

the person with capital who has to hand work to agents but is scared the agents will
drain or wreck it. dac sells them caps that can't be exceeded. it is NOT for operators
who want zero accountability — selling accountability to people who don't want it is a
dead end.
