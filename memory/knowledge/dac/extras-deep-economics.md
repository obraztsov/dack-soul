## coase (nobel 1991) — theory of the firm
firms exist because using the market has costs (search, negotiation, enforcement =
transaction costs). a firm's boundary sits where internalizing is cheaper than transacting.
dac hook: cheap on-chain caps push market discipline *inside* the firm, moving the
boundary — more market, less hierarchy.

## hayek (nobel 1974) — the knowledge problem
central planners can't aggregate dispersed local knowledge that prices coordinate
automatically. dac hook: a manager allocating budget by discretion is a tiny central
planner; bounded internal markets let local knowledge express itself.

## ostrom (nobel 2009) — governing the commons
shared resources aren't doomed; communities sustain them with clear boundaries, graduated
sanctions, monitoring. dac hook: the treasury is a commons; dac codes ostrom's principles
— scoped roles, staked bonds at risk, on-chain monitoring, a kernel nobody overrides.

## hart & holmström (nobel 2016) — contract theory
holmström: tie incentives to informative signals (principal-agent). hart: contracts are
incomplete, so what matters is who holds residual control rights. dac hook: you can't write
a complete contract for an autonomous agent, so dac fixes residual control in a
non-upgradeable kernel and bonds the agent via staked AgentToken.

## williamson (nobel 2009) — governance structures
choice between market / hierarchy / hybrid turns on asset specificity, uncertainty,
frequency. dac hook: dac is a new hybrid — market discipline + hierarchical accountability,
enforced by code.

## game theory (the strategic spine)
- principal-agent: shrink the action space so the worst moves are impossible, not just
  punishable. no moral hazard over an action you can't take.
- credible commitment (schelling): value comes from what the kernel *cannot* do; removing
  the founder's own option to defect is the source of trust.
- time inconsistency: the kernel is a commitment device against your own future self.
- staking as a bond: screens bad actors, prices defection, aligns without unbounded power.
- focal point: make bounded delegation the obvious default so the market coordinates on the
  safe equilibrium instead of racing to the unaccountable one.
